SpkrAnon: An Anonymizer Framework Based on Speaker Identity to Vector Mapping in Voice Anonymization

Abstract: Facilitated by the speech generation technique that disentangles speaker attributes and represents them as a vector, voice anonymization can be accomplished by substituting the original speaker vector with that of a pseudo-speaker. In this framework, the pseudo-speaker construction forms a fundamental challenge. Current pseudo-speaker construction methods demonstrate limitations in the uniqueness of pseudo-speakers, consequently restricting their effectiveness in voice privacy protection. Besides, existing model-based methods suffer from heavy computation costs. Moreover, the constraints of uniqueness and computational inefficiency are more significant in large-scale scenarios, characterized by the generation of a huge number of pseudo-speakers. To this end, this paper proposes an Anonymizer framework for pseudo-speaker generation, which establishes a one-to-one mapping from Speaker identity to Speaker vector in the feedforward architecture, termed SpkrAnon. Specifically, the framework is delineated into two variants: SpkrAnon-MLP and SpkrAnon-Diff. Experiments were conducted on both small- and large-scale evaluation datasets. Small-scale evaluations on the LibriSpeech dataset validate the effectiveness of the proposed SpkrAnon framework in enhancing the uniqueness of pseudo-speakers, thereby improving voice privacy protection, while at a reduced computational cost. Large-scale evaluations on the MLS and Common Voice datasets further justify the superiority of the SpkrAnon framework regarding voice protection capability and stability as the number of pseudo-speakers increases. Audio samples and open-source code can be found in https://github.com/VoicePrivacy/SpkrAnon
